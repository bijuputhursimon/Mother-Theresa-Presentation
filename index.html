Lessons from St. Mother Teresa

Faith, Love, and Simplicity

Biju & Men's Group

Who Was Mother Teresa?

  • Albanian nun who founded the Missionaries of Charity in 1950.
  • Dedicated her life to the "poorest of the poor" in Calcutta.
  • Nobel Peace Prize winner (1979) and Canonized Saint (2016).
  • Driven by the cry of Jesus on the Cross: "I thirst."

Spiritual Poverty in the West

"The poverty in the West is a poverty of loneliness and spirituality. There's a hunger for love, as there is a hunger for God."

The Cure: Love and Presence

Material goods cannot fill a soul created for God.

  • Presence is more valuable than "providing."
  • As men, do we give our families our *stuff* or our *selves*?

Simplicity: Path to Joy

"Live simply so others may simply live."

Her life was a protest against the "clutter" of the world that distracts us from Christ.

Sharing vs. Giving

True love involves sacrifice. It isn't giving from abundance; it's giving from need.

The Dark Night: Heroic Faith

She served for 50 years without "feeling" God's presence.

  • Faith is an act of the **will**, not an emotion.
  • Staying the course when it feels empty is true spiritual grit.

Leadership from Conviction

She was often called "difficult" because she was unshakeable.

  • She trusted God's voice over human "practicality."
  • Real leadership requires a "Holy Stubbornness."

Entrepreneurial Faith

She stepped out of the convent with no money, no plan, only Providence.

Lesson: When God calls, the "business plan" follows the action.

Pure Love, No Force

She served Hindus and Muslims with the same dignity as Christians.

"I would not do this without Christ... but we support everyone's faith."

Embracing Suffering

She believed suffering unites us to the Cross of Christ.

She didn't just provide medical care; she provided a **dignified death** to those dying in the streets.

Defending the Vulnerable

She stood before world leaders to defend the unborn.

Closing: Call to Action

"Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love."

Challenge: Simplify one thing this week. Reach out to one lonely person.
